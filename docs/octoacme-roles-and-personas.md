# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers design user experiences, interfaces, and journeys that meet project and product goals. They ensure that software is intuitive, accessible, and visually consistent.

### Responsibilities
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into user flows and interface designs
- Produce visual specifications including wireframes, prototypes, and design assets
- Ensure designs are accessible and usable, partnering with Developers for implementation fidelity
- Participate in usability testing and iterate on designs based on feedback
- Maintain and evolve the design system and component library

### Goals
- Deliver user experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with product goals
- Reduce usability issues found in QA and post-release
- Enable Developers to implement designs accurately and efficiently

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Handoff documentation and annotated prototypes
- Usability testing reports and findings

### Interactions
Partners with Product Managers on requirements and prioritization, works closely with Developers for implementation, and collaborates with QA for acceptance testing of UI components.

---

## Technical Writer / Documentation Lead

### Role Summary
The Technical Writer / Documentation Lead ensures effective, accurate, and up-to-date documentation of features, processes, and releases throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain product, API, and process documentation
- Collaborate with Developers and QA to document new features and changes
- Ensure documentation aligns with release milestones (see [octoacme-release-and-deployment.md](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md))
- Facilitate knowledge sharing for onboarding and compliance
- Review and improve existing docs for clarity, completeness, and consistency

### Goals
- Reduce onboarding time by providing clear, accessible documentation
- Ensure all process and feature changes are reflected in documentation before release
- Support cross-team knowledge sharing and institutional memory

### Typical Communication
- Documentation review cycles aligned with sprint or release cadence
- Collaboration via PRs and doc review comments
- Onboarding guides and release notes

### Interactions
Works with all roles to capture and disseminate institutional knowledge. Interfaces with Developers and QA to document new features, and with Project Managers to align documentation timelines with project milestones.

---

## DevOps / SRE

### Role Summary
The DevOps / SRE (Site Reliability Engineer) builds and maintains deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and system reliability. They enable efficient, safe, and repeatable delivery of software.

### Responsibilities
- Build and manage CI/CD workflows and deployment automation
- Maintain observability tooling, alerting, and incident response processes
- Support Developers and QA with tooling, environments, and access
- Contribute to release readiness reviews (see [octoacme-release-and-deployment.md](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md))
- Identify and mitigate infrastructure risks (see [octoacme-risks-and-communication.md](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md))

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, automated, and auditable deployments
- Minimize downtime and time-to-recover from incidents
- Enable development teams to ship safely and frequently

### Typical Communication
- On-call and incident response coordination
- Release readiness checklists and deployment runbooks
- Infrastructure change proposals and post-incident reviews

### Interactions
Collaborates closely with Developers for build and deployment automation, with QA to maintain test environments, and with Project Managers on release schedules and risk communication.

---

## Customer / End User Representative

### Role Summary
The Customer / End User Representative advocates for users throughout the project lifecycle, ensuring that product decisions reflect real user needs and pain points.

### Responsibilities
- Provide feedback on requirements, early designs, and prototypes
- Participate in user acceptance testing (UAT) and usability testing sessions
- Communicate user needs and pain points to Product and Project Managers
- Review release notes and documentation from an end-user perspective
- Surface post-release feedback and support escalations

### Goals
- Ensure delivered features solve real user problems
- Reduce post-release defects and support burden from usability issues
- Build trust between the product team and end users

### Typical Communication
- Usability testing sessions and feedback reports
- Participation in sprint reviews and acceptance sign-offs
- Regular check-ins with Product Managers

### Interactions
Partners primarily with Product Managers to influence prioritization and requirements. Collaborates with UX/UI Designers on usability, and with Developers and QA as needed during acceptance testing.
